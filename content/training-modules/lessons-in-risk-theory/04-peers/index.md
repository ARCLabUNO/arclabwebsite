<div class="arc-module-hero">
  <div class="arc-module-kicker">Module 4 · Risk Application</div>
  <h2>Behavior is influenced by the people around us.</h2>
  <p>
    One of the strongest criminogenic needs is antisocial peers. In fact, many probation
    and parole conditions prohibit associating with known justice-involved individuals.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-thesis">
  <strong>Key takeaway</strong>
  <p>Who we spend time with helps shape what behaviors become normal.</p>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>Why Peers Matter</h2>

  <p>
    Peers can affect:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>attitudes</li>
    <li>decision-making</li>
    <li>opportunities</li>
    <li>reinforcement of behavior</li>
  </ul>

  <div class="arc-module-key">
    <strong>The idea:</strong> Social environments influence behavior.
  </div>
</div>

{{< antisocial-peers-figure >}}

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>How Antisocial Peers Shape Behavior</h2>

  <p>
    When people spend time with antisocial peers, criminal behavior can become:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>normalized</li>
    <li>encouraged</li>
    <li>rewarded</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>How Prosocial Peers Help</h2>

  <p>
    At the same time, prosocial peers can provide:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>support</li>
    <li>accountability</li>
    <li>alternative opportunities</li>
    <li>positive role models</li>
  </ul>

  <p>
    This is one reason peer associations are included in many needs assessments.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>Why This Is Dynamic</h2>

  <p>
    Unlike criminal history, peer networks are dynamic. Changing who someone spends time
    with can alter the opportunities, reinforcement, and social norms that shape behavior.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>Why This Matters</h2>

  <ul>
    <li>Social environments influence behavior</li>
    <li>Peer associations can change over time</li>
    <li>Effective interventions often focus on building prosocial networks</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-bottom">
  <h2>Bottom Line</h2>
  <p>
    Who we spend time with helps shape what behaviors become normal. Peer associations
    matter because they can reinforce risk or support change.
  </p>
</div>
