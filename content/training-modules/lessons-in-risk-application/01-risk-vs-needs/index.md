<div class="arc-module-hero">
  <div class="arc-module-kicker">Module 1 · Risk Needs Tool Lessons</div>
  <h2>Prediction is not the same as intervention.</h2>
  <p>
    Risk assessment and needs assessment are related, but they are not the same thing.
    One asks how likely an outcome is. The other asks what factors should be targeted
    for intervention.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-thesis">
  <strong>Key takeaway</strong>
  <p>Good prediction does not automatically tell us what to treat.</p>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>What Risk Assessment Asks</h2>

  <p>
    A risk assessment asks:
  </p>

  <div class="arc-module-key">
    <strong>The question:</strong> How likely is an outcome?
  </div>

  <p>
    Examples include:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>recidivism</li>
    <li>violence</li>
    <li>failure to appear</li>
  </ul>

  <p>
    These tools are often used to inform:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>supervision intensity</li>
    <li>classification</li>
    <li>release decisions</li>
  </ul>
</div>

{{< risk-vs-needs-figure >}}

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>What Needs Assessment Asks</h2>

  <p>
    A needs assessment asks a different question:
  </p>

  <div class="arc-module-key">
    <strong>The question:</strong> What factors should we target for intervention?
  </div>

  <p>
    Examples include:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>substance use</li>
    <li>peer associations</li>
    <li>employment</li>
    <li>antisocial cognition</li>
  </ul>

  <p>
    These tools are used to guide:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>treatment planning</li>
    <li>programming</li>
    <li>case management</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>Some Factors Do Both</h2>

  <p>
    Some factors do both. Dynamic criminogenic needs can:
  </p>

  <ul>
    <li>predict recidivism</li>
    <li>serve as intervention targets</li>
  </ul>

  <p>
    That overlap matters, but it does not make prediction and intervention the same thing.
  </p>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-section">
  <h2>Why This Matters</h2>

  <ul>
    <li>Prediction is not the same as intervention</li>
    <li>Some strong predictors are not easily changeable</li>
    <li>Effective correctional practice requires both risk and needs assessment</li>
  </ul>
</div>

<div class="arc-module-bottom">
  <h2>Bottom Line</h2>
  <p>
    Good prediction does not automatically tell us what to treat. Risk assessment helps
    estimate likelihood; needs assessment helps identify intervention targets.
  </p>
</div>
