Training Modules are short, focused explainers designed to clarify how commonly used tools and concepts work in practice.

They are built for practitioners, students, and instructors who work with corrections-related data, decisions, and policies. Importantly, they are for people who need to understand what these tools actually do, not just what they produce.

Each module:
- focuses on a single concept  
- uses simple visuals or examples  
- connects directly to real-world decision contexts  

---

## Current Series

### Risk Assessment

Risk-needs assessment tools are used every day in corrections, pretrial decision-making, and classification systems.

They shape decisions about incarceration, supervision, and programming.

But one basic issue is often overlooked:

**Not all risk scores mean the same thing.**

Some tools clearly distinguish between higher- and lower-risk individuals. Others barely do—yet both are often used as if they were equally informative.

This series breaks down how these tools actually work, how they are evaluated, and how to interpret their outputs in practice.

👉 Start here: [Why Risk Scores Are Misunderstood](/training-modules/risk-assessment/)
